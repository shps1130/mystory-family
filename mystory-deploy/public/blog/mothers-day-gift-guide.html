How to Give Your Mom a Gift She'll Actually Use This Mother's Day

By MyStory.Family · April 2025 · 5 min read

Every year, the same question.

What do you get the woman who has everything she needs and wants nothing that takes up space?

You've done the flowers. You've done the brunch. You've done the gift card she used on groceries and the candle that's still sitting on her bathroom shelf, unlit.

This year, something is different. You find yourself thinking — really thinking — about your mom. About how much she has lived. How much she has carried. How much she knows that you don't know yet. And how little of it has ever been written down.

That feeling is pointing you somewhere. And this post is about following it.

Why most Mother's Day gifts don't last

It's not that flowers aren't beautiful or that brunch isn't lovely. It's that they're designed to be temporary. They're meant for the day — for the moment — and then they're gone.

Which is fine, most of the time. But there's a growing awareness in a lot of adult children right now — a quiet urgency that most of us don't talk about out loud — that the window for some things is closing.

Your mom won't always be here. Her memories won't always be sharp. The stories she carries — the ones she's never told, or told only once, or told so many times you stopped really listening — those stories are finite.

A gift that helps preserve them is not just a nice gesture. It's one of the most meaningful things you could do this year.

What your mom actually wants

Ask most mothers what they want for Mother's Day and they'll say "nothing" or "just to spend time with you." But underneath that is something more specific.

They want to feel that their life mattered. That the things they sacrificed were noticed. That the wisdom they've gathered over a lifetime is worth something to the people they love most.

They want to be known — not just as Mom, but as the person they were before that title. The young woman who had dreams. The person who made hard decisions. The believer who held on through seasons that nearly broke her.

A life story gives her a way to share all of that. Not in one overwhelming conversation. Not by sitting down and trying to write a book. Just one memory at a time, in her own words, at her own pace.

A few questions worth asking yourself

Before you decide what to give this year, sit with these for a moment:

  • Do you know the story of how your mom became who she is? Not the highlight reel — the real story. The hard years. The defining moments. The faith that carried her when nothing else did.
  • Do your kids know who their grandmother really is? Not just Grandma — the woman with a full life before that title.
  • If your mom passed away tomorrow, what story would be lost forever? That question is uncomfortable. It is also clarifying.

If any of those questions gave you pause, that is the gift. Give her a way to answer them — in her own words, at her own pace, before the window closes.

This Mother's Day is different

You can feel it, can't you? The awareness that time is moving. That the conversations you've been meaning to have are still waiting. That the woman who gave you everything deserves more than another arrangement of flowers that will be in the trash by Thursday.

She deserves to be known. She deserves to have her story told.
