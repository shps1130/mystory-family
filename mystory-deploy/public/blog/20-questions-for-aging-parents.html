20 Questions to Ask Your Aging Parents Before It's Too Late

By MyStory.Family · April 2025 · 5 min read

Most of us think we know our parents.

We know their habits, their preferences, their opinions on politics and grandchildren and the right way to make a pie crust. We've had hundreds of dinners, thousands of conversations.

But here's what most of us don't know: the story of who they were before we existed. The story of what they believed in the quiet. The story of what they endured and what they carried and what they were trusting God for when nobody was watching.

These 20 questions are designed to open those doors. Use them at a family dinner. On a car ride. During a quiet afternoon. You don't need to ask all of them at once — one great question is worth more than twenty rushed ones.

About Their Early Life

  1. What did your childhood home look like? Describe it room by room — what do you remember most?
  2. Who was the most important person in your life when you were young, and why?
  3. What did you dream about becoming when you were a child? How did those dreams change?
  4. What was the hardest part of growing up in your family?
  5. What is something from your childhood that shaped who you became — even if you didn't realize it at the time?

About the Defining Seasons

  1. What was the hardest year of your life? How did you get through it?
  2. Was there a moment when everything changed? What happened?
  3. What decision are you most grateful you made — and what do you wish you'd decided differently?
  4. What season of your life taught you the most?
  5. Was there a time you almost gave up on something important? What kept you going?

About Faith and Meaning

  1. When did faith become real to you — not just something you believed, but something you lived?
  2. What is the most significant prayer you have ever prayed? How was it answered?
  3. Has there ever been a season when you doubted? What brought you back?
  4. What do you believe God has been faithful to you about — something you can point to and say, "I saw him there"?
  5. What are you still trusting God for?

About Love and Family

  1. What do you most want your grandchildren to know about you — the real you, not just the grandparent version?
  2. What did you learn about love that took you the longest to understand?
  3. What are you most proud of that nobody ever gave you credit for?
  4. What do you hope people say about you after you're gone?
  5. What is the one thing you most want to make sure we never forget?

What to do with the answers

Write them down. Record the conversation on your phone — most people forget it's even running. Take notes afterward while things are fresh.
